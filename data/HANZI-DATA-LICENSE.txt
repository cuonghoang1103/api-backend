Stroke data for Han characters
==============================

hanzi-graphics-ja.txt.gz  (Japanese kanji, 7,007 characters)
  Source : animCJK — https://github.com/parsimonhi/animCJK  (graphicsJa.txt)
  Licence: ARPHIC PUBLIC LICENSE
  Note   : gzip of the upstream file, byte-for-byte. Not modified.
  Why not npm: hanzi-writer-data (npm) is built from Chinese forms and has no
  Japanese shinjitai — 気, 読 and 毎 are simply absent, and using their Chinese
  ancestors (氣, 讀, 每) would teach the wrong character. hanzi-writer-data-jp
  exists on npm but ships no data at all (47KB, 12 files, stub).

Chinese hanzi come from the `hanzi-writer-data` npm package (same licence),
which is installed as a dependency rather than vendored here.

The renderer, hanzi-writer, is MIT.
